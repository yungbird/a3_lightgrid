To understand how light works, we first need to understand the Electromagnetic (or EM) spectrum.

The EM spectrum is the spectrum on which all forms of radiation energy occur on. This spectrum includes energy such as x-ray radiation, microwave radiation, and radio waves

The light we see falls on a very small part of the EM spectrum, called the Visible Light spectrum.


DeMichele, 2016

It occurs between Ultraviolet radiation, and Infrared radiation. Visible light radiation is the only radiation our eyes are biologically designed to receive.

Each colour we see around us can be found on the visible light spectrum, occurring at a different wavelength. On one end of the spectrum, there is violet light - light which has a short wavelength at higher frequencies. At the other end, there is red light - light which has a high wavelength at lower frequencies.

This is easy to remember, as violet light occurs just after Ultraviolet light on the EM spectrum, while red light occurs just before Infrared light.


Crowellphoto.com, 2014

Where is pink? Good question!

Colours such as pink, magenta, and fuchsia do not actually appear on the visible light spectrum and don't have a wavelength! That's not to say they don't exist though - they're simply our brain's way of bridging the gap between red and violet.
